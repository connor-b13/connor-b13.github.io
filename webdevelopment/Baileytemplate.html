Hello, world

My interest in technology stems from a desire to solve tangible problems and build things that last. Coming from a humble background I learned early on the value of hard work, discipline, and attention to detail. Transitioning into computer science allowed me to apply those same values into programming and machine learning, where efficient code and thoughtful architecture can streamline complex processes and impact lives. Coming to Mizzou, I have been able to learn the technical skills necessary to support my previous values. I have had the opportunity to work with teams of people and to make connections. From my work with machine learning models and with database management at Shaffer & Associates, I have learned how critical data integrity and system design are. These experiences reinforced my belief that building software isn't just about making things work, but about making them reliable, secure, and scalable. I have tried to maintain integrity in everything that I do. From being consistent in the gym to perfecting my programming practices, I learned early on that there is value in doing things the hard way. By challenging myself in all aspects of my life I have opened many doors towards greater opportunities. I may never be satisfied with the progress I have made, but I can recognize the hard work I have put into shaping my career and my future. As I approach the final two semesters of my college education, my eyes are now set towards networking, internships and potential career paths. I have accomplished much during my time in college, but I feel that I have only scratched the surface of my potential.
